Released in 1999, Resident Evil 3 Nemesis follows former S.T.A.R.S. member Jill Valentine as she attempts a desperate escape from a crumbling Raccoon City. Set both 24 hours before and after the events of the second game, Jill navigates the viral outbreak while being relentlessly pursued by the Nemesis an intelligent, T Virus powered bioweapon programmed specifically to eliminate the surviving police officers who know Umbrellas secrets. Along the way, she teams up with Carlos Oliveira and a squad of Umbrella Biohazard Countermeasure Service U.B.C.S. mercenaries, eventually discovering that the U.S. government plans to sterilize the city with a nuclear strike to contain the infection.

The game introduced a Live Selection system, where players had to make split second decisions that altered the story path, and a crafting mechanic for gunpowder. Unlike its predecessors, Nemesis focused more on frantic action and the constant, suffocating dread of an invincible stalker who could follow you through doors terrifying first for the series.

Why the Original Outshines the Remake
While the 2020 remake has modern graphics, many fans consider it a significant step down from the 1999 masterpiece for several key reasons

Cut Content The remake infamously slashed huge portions of the original game. Iconic locations like the Raccoon City Park, the Dead Factory, and the Graveyard along with the Grave Digger boss fight were removed entirely.

The Nemesis Encounter In the original, Nemesis was a persistent, unpredictable threat that felt like he was hunting you. In the remake, his appearances are largely scripted sequences or on rails chases, stripping away the organic tension.

Live Selection and Replayability The 1999 version featured multiple endings and randomized item and enemy placements based on your choices. The remake is a linear, one and done experience that removed the choice system altogether.

Atmosphere and Pacing The original felt like a wide scale urban disaster. The remake feels like a condensed greatest hits reel that rushes the player toward the finish line in about five or six hours.

Fun Fact In the original game, Nemesis was the first enemy in the series capable of following the player through loading screens doors, shattering the safe zone logic players had relied on in previous titles.
